Chao-Tsung Huang received the B.S. degree from Department of Electrical Engineering, NTU, in 2001, and the Ph.D. degree from Graduate Institute of Electronics Engineering, NTU, in 2005. He is now with NTHU, Taiwan, as an assistant professor.bFrom 2005 to 2011, he worked for Novatek Microelectronics Corp., Taiwan, as an IC designer for SoC products and a team leader responsible for developing multi-standard image and video codecs. He performed postdoctoral research on the first HEVC decoder chip at Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Cambridge, from March 2011 to August 2012. He then worked on light-field camera design as his postdoctoral research at NTU, Taiwan, until July 2013. His research interests include light-field cameras and high performance video codec, especially from algorithm exploration to VLSI architecture design, chip implementation, and demo system. He received the MediaTek Fellowship from 2003 to 2005. He serves as an Associate Editor of Springer Circuits, Systems and Signal Processing.
